1. What are you building? Who is it for? 
- A recipe finder web app where users can search for recipes by ingredients, cuisine, or dish name. It is for anyone who wants a quick meal inspiration without scrolling through many different food blogs. 

2. Why? What motivates you to build this? What problem does it solve?
- Finding a recipe that matches what someone actually has at home is not always possible. This app will cut through straight to the point and results...no ads or anything, just the recipes. 

3. MVP vs. Stretch Goals: What is the minimum working version? What would you add if you have time?
- MVP:
    - search bar to loop up recipes by keyword or ingredient
    - display results with recipe name, image, and key infor (cook time, servings, etc...)
    - click through to full recipes details 
- Stretch Goals:
    - filter by dietary restrictions
    - save/favorite recipes
    - "what is in my fridge?" mode (you can input multiple ingredients and get matching recipes)

4. What technologies do you plan to use? APIs, libraries, frameworks, etc.
- HTML/CSS/JS
- fetch API to handle API calls 
- and a vanilla JS to keep it simple
